The Eighth Floor

Some dreams don’t scream. They whisper.

You ever wake up from a dream that feels… more like a warning than a dream? Like your mind wasn’t just imagining things—but showing you something?

Some places don't scream danger. They whisper it. Soft enough that only your bones hear it first.

It started as a getaway. Me, a few friends, the open road. We found it deep in the woods: a hotel—tall, pristine, wrapped in vines and gold light. Seven floors, the clerk said.

When I saw the building, something in my stomach turned. Not fear. Something worse. Knowing.

But I ignored it. We checked in. Laughed. Took pictures. Believed what we wanted to believe.

Later, I returned to the lobby to get my luggage. Pressed the elevator button. Saw something strange. Seven floor buttons… and one more: G for Ground.

No one mentioned a ground floor. Curiosity won. I pressed it.

The doors opened with a soft hiss. And everything changed.

The hallway was yellow. Sickly. The air smelled metallic. And then I saw them—children in giant test tubes. Floating. Silent. Not dead, not alive. Doll-like.

These weren’t ordinary children. These were the different. The ones society tried to erase—Autism. ADHD. Beautiful minds deemed wrong.

And they were all brown. Black. But their skin... pale. Blond. False.

Cracked porcelain over living faces.

The realization hit: this place wasn’t luxury. It was erasure. A system disguised as care. A machine for making people disappear beneath politeness, beneath kindness, beneath lies.

Seven floors for comfort. An eighth for cruelty.

I stood frozen. Not because I didn’t understand—but because I did. This wasn’t just a dream. This was a truth. One the world hides under soft lighting and floral wallpapers.

I stepped back into the elevator. The door slid shut like a secret being kept.

When I returned upstairs, nothing had changed. My friends still laughed. The stars still blinked. The hotel still glowed. But now I knew.

There are hotels like this everywhere.

Places built on the silence of the different. Places that conform, correct, erase.

They don’t look like evil. Because they never have to.
